Traditional French Baguette

OVERVIEW
The iconic French baguette - crispy crust, open crumb, wheaty flavor.
Uses poolish pre-ferment for complexity and extensibility.

POOLISH (12 hours before)
- 250g bread flour
- 250g water
- 0.1g instant yeast (tiny pinch)
Mix well, cover, ferment 12 hours at room temperature.

FINAL DOUGH
1. Combine poolish with remaining 250g flour, 100g water, 5g yeast, 10g salt, malt.
2. Mix on low 3 minutes, then medium 5-6 minutes.
3. Dough should be smooth, moderately strong, slightly tacky.

BULK FERMENTATION
1. Place in lightly oiled container.
2. Give one set of folds at 45 minutes.
3. Total bulk: 90 minutes at 75°F.

DIVIDING AND PRE-SHAPING
1. Divide dough into 3 pieces (~370g each).
2. Pre-shape loosely into short cylinders.
3. Rest seam-side up on floured couche, 15-20 minutes.

SHAPING BAGUETTES
1. Gently flatten pre-shaped piece.
2. Fold top third down, bottom third up (like a letter).
3. Roll with hands from center outward to ~16 inches.
4. Taper ends gently.
5. Place on floured couche between folds to hold shape.

FINAL PROOF
45-60 minutes at room temperature. Dough should spring back slowly when poked.

BAKING (with steam)
1. Preheat oven and baking stone to 480°F for 1 hour.
2. Score each baguette 5-7 times at 30-degree angle, overlapping by 1/3.
3. Load onto stone, add steam immediately (1 cup boiling water in pan below).
4. Bake 22-25 minutes. Vent steam at 10 minutes by opening door briefly.
5. Baguettes should be deep golden with caramelized scores.
6. Cool on rack at least 30 minutes.
