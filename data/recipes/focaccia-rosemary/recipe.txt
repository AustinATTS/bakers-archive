Rosemary Focaccia

OVERVIEW
A pillowy, olive-oil rich Italian flatbread with crispy bottom and dimpled top.
High hydration dough, long cold proof for maximum flavor.

DOUGH (Day Before)
1. Whisk yeast into 400ml lukewarm water. Let sit 5 minutes.
2. Add flour and salt, mix until no dry flour remains (2-3 minutes).
3. Dough will be very sticky - that's normal.
4. Drizzle 2 tbsp olive oil over dough and fold over itself.
5. Cover and refrigerate 12-24 hours.

NEXT DAY - PAN PREP
1. Pour 3 tbsp olive oil into 9x13 inch baking pan.
2. Transfer cold dough to pan, turn to coat in oil.
3. Cover, let rest at room temperature 3-4 hours.
4. Dough should spread to fill pan and become very bubbly.

DIMPLING AND TOPPING
1. Pour remaining 1 tbsp oil over surface.
2. Using all 10 fingers, dimple dough deeply and vigorously.
3. Scatter rosemary leaves over surface.
4. Sprinkle generously with flaky salt.

BAKING
1. Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C).
2. Bake 25-30 minutes until deep golden on top and bottom.
3. Check bottom by lifting edge - should be crisp and golden.
4. Cool in pan 5 minutes, then transfer to rack.
5. Best served warm or at room temperature same day.

SERVING SUGGESTIONS
- Tear into pieces, serve with olive oil and balsamic
- Use as sandwich bread
- Top with cherry tomatoes before baking for variation
