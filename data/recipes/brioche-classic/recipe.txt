Classic Brioche

OVERVIEW
A rich, buttery French bread with tender golden crumb. Requires a stand mixer 
and patience - the butter incorporation takes time and cannot be rushed.

INGREDIENTS
- 500g bread flour
- 4 tsp instant yeast
- 1.5 tsp salt
- 3 tbsp sugar
- 5 large eggs, room temperature
- 250g unsalted butter, softened to cool room temperature
- 2 tbsp whole milk
- Egg wash: 1 egg + 1 tbsp milk, beaten

INSTRUCTIONS

MIXING - DOUGH BASE
1. In stand mixer with dough hook, combine flour, yeast, salt, sugar.
2. Add eggs and milk, mix on low until dough comes together (3-4 min).
3. Increase to medium, knead until dough is smooth and pulling from sides (8-10 min).
4. Dough should be strong and pass windowpane test.

BUTTER INCORPORATION (the key step)
1. With mixer on medium-low, add butter 2 tbsp at a time.
2. Wait until each addition is fully incorporated before adding more.
3. This takes 15-20 minutes total - do not rush or dough will break.
4. Final dough should be glossy, smooth, and pull cleanly from bowl.

FIRST RISE
1. Transfer to lightly buttered bowl.
2. Cover and refrigerate 8-16 hours (cold ferment develops flavor).
3. Dough will roughly double.

SHAPING (Loaf Method)
1. Remove cold dough and divide into 8 equal pieces.
2. Roll each into smooth ball.
3. Place 4 balls in each buttered 9x5 loaf pan.
4. Cover loosely with plastic wrap.

SECOND RISE
2-4 hours at room temperature until dough rises to just above pan rim.
(Cold dough takes longer to warm up.)

BAKING
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
2. Brush gently with egg wash (don't let it drip down sides).
3. Bake 30-35 minutes until deep golden brown.
4. Internal temperature: 190°F.
5. Cool in pan 10 minutes, then on rack.
6. Wait at least 1 hour before cutting for best texture.
